PHASE 1: ORDER MATTERS

Phase 1 is all about how algorithms are all around us in our everyday lives. As children learn new school routines at the beginning of the year, Phase 1 will help them understand sequencing and make connections to computer science.

Powerful Ideas

  • Algorithms
  • Hardware/Software
  • Modularity

Learning Standards

  • PK.AL.SR.7 Reflect and plan a logical series of steps to accomplish a task, such as writing a message, completing a puzzle, drawing a picture, or building a block structure.
  • PK.08 Use directions such as up, down, in front, and behind.
  • PK.PD.2 Demonstrate awareness of spatial boundaries and the ability to work and move within them.
  • PK.SL.PKI.4 Describe familiar people, places, things, and events, and, with prompting and support, provide additional detail.

Goal

Children will understand that using steps (as in algorithms) helps us do tasks & solve problems in computer science, literature, and in daily life. Sometimes the order of steps when sequencing matters; other times, order does not matter.

Phase 1 Timeline

Each CRRAFT phase lasts approximately 6 weeks. Phases can and should overlap or be extended or shortened as needed in your classroom. This timeline is a suggestion.

Things to Consider

Cultural Relevance: Celebrate differences! Discuss at-home routines (brushing teeth, making sandwiches; driving/walking to school). Routines can vary from family to family, be sure to celebrate these differences.

Order Matters: Discuss why the order of steps is important in at-home routines. Consider times when the order may not matter as much. Recall algorithms for at-home tasks without actually doing them.

Essential Questions

  • What is a sequence? What is important to know about sequences?
  • How are coding sequences enacted (with robotics)?
  • How do we use sequences to make sense of the world around us? How do we make sense of sequences?

Centers Materials & Ideas

  • Art: Laminated directional cards, recycled materials for maze building. In art, we can build mazes - Design Process.
  • Blocks: Robot mice, directional cards, numbered velcro strips for directional cards, various blocks, and loose parts for students to create mazes for mice, Magna Tiles. In blocks, we can explore Robot Mice - Modularity, Hardware/Software, Sequences and Algorithms.
  • Books/Library: Books on coding (How to Code a Sandcastle), sequencing books (If You Give a Mouse a Cookie, There Was an Old Lady who Swallowed a Fly), routine-based books (Otto Goes to School). In the library, we can learn about sequencing - Sequences and Algorithms.
  • Dramatic Play: Dress up materials for nursery rhymes, If You Give a Mouse a Cookie props, recipe cards and cookbooks. In dramatic play, we can act out routines, recipes, and stories - Representation.
  • Puzzles and Games: Robot Mouse board game. In puzzles/games, we can play a game to learn about algorithms - Sequences and Algorithms, Debugging.
  • iPad/Computer: Think & Learn Code-a-Pillar App, PBS kids games (Routines Games). In iPad/computer, we can explore coding and learn more routines - Sequences and Algorithms.
  • Writing: Paper and copies of directional cards, book making materials, story sequencing pages. In writing, we can write stories, write books, and write directions - Representation.

Small Groups

  • Sequencing a Routine: Students will discuss and order the steps to wash hands. Standards: PK.AL.SR.7 Reflect and plan a logical series of steps to accomplish a task.
  • Write an "If" Story: Students will discuss and order the If You Give a Mouse... story events and write their own version. Standards: With prompting and support, orally retell familiar stories including details.
  • Linear and Nonlinear: Students will consider linear & nonlinear narratives and construct their own. Standards: PK.W.TTP.3 With modeling, prompting, and support, use a combination of drawing, dictating, and/or emergent writing to narrate a single event.
  • Mouse Program: Students will explore Robot Mouse and program the mouse to get the cookie. Standards: PK.AL.SR.7 Reflect and plan a logical series of steps to accomplish a task.

Optional Focal Experience: Sequence the Day

Objective: Students will collaborate to co-construct, sequence, and represent the classroom daily schedule.

Materials Needed: Routine book (e.g., Otto Goes to School, Hello/Buenos Días, or another), chart paper, markers.

Key Vocabulary: Algorithm, Order, Sequential words (first, then, next).

Focal Experience: Program the Teacher

Objective: Students will work together to develop a program to code their teacher to move to achieve a particular goal.

Materials Needed: If You Give a Mouse a Cookie, Foam Mats.
