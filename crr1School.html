What to Expect

The CRR program is designed to help you engage children in computer science activities during class. There are 4 phases in the program that engage children with

7 powerful ideas of computer science.
The phased approach gradually emphasizes these powerful ideas explicitly, but children are engaging with ALL powerful ideas in every phase. Each phase takes about 6 weeks and includes a focal experience to emphasize powerful ideas in computer science. You can do the focal experience any time during the phase, and you choose the lead up activities to get you and your students ready for the experience! We encourage you to use materials flexibly so that the program works for you and your students.

Phase 1

Discover the CRRAFT Phase 1 Curriculum

The "Phase 1 Working Draft" of the CRRAFT Partnership School Curriculum, titled "Order Matters", is an engaging educational resource designed to introduce young students to foundational concepts in computer science, such as sequencing, algorithms, and modularity.

Through interactive activities, such as using robot mice and coding cards, children learn to see everyday routines as algorithms, understanding that the order of steps can sometimes be crucial. The curriculum emphasizes hands-on learning with a variety of materials, including books, puzzles, and building blocks, to help students grasp these important ideas.

This draft integrates cultural relevance and non-linear storytelling, making it an inclusive and comprehensive guide for educators aiming to foster early computational thinking in their classrooms. It is an excellent tool for teachers to help students connect daily life experiences with computer science, fostering curiosity and problem-solving skills from an early age.

Phase 2

Explore the CRRAFT Phase 2 Curriculum

The "Phase 2 Working Draft" of the CRRAFT Partnership School Curriculum, titled "We Can Do STEM", is an innovative educational resource designed to deepen students' understanding of STEM concepts through strategic problem-solving.

In Phase 2, students are introduced to the Design Process, a structured approach to tackling complex challenges by planning, creating prototypes, and debugging. This phase emphasizes the interaction between hardware and software, helping students learn how robots work and how to communicate with them.

Through hands-on activities such as building robots, coding, and designing solutions, children are encouraged to see that STEM is accessible to everyone and can be applied to everyday problems. The curriculum integrates cultural relevance and supports cyclical problem-solving, recognizing that issues can be revisited and refined over time.

This draft is an engaging and inclusive guide for educators, providing a wealth of resources, including robot kits, art supplies, and interactive centers, to help students develop their creativity, collaboration, and critical thinking skills. Phase 2 is designed to inspire young learners to see themselves as capable problem-solvers who can use STEM to make a positive impact in their communities.
