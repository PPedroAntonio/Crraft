Overview

Phase 3 of the CRRAFT Partnership is focused on helping students understand that they can accomplish big things by breaking down complex tasks into smaller, manageable steps. This phase emphasizes the use of modularity, debugging, and algorithms to approach problem-solving in a structured way.

The goal is to build a comprehensive understanding of how to solve complex problems by breaking them into smaller units and combining those units to achieve the larger goal. The curriculum is designed to be flexible, allowing phases to overlap, extend, or shorten based on classroom needs.

Powerful Ideas

Phase 3 emphasizes three powerful ideas that are central to the curriculum:

  • Modularity: Breaking down complex tasks into simpler, more manageable units.
  • Debugging: Identifying and solving problems by focusing on smaller steps.
  • Algorithms: Creating step-by-step procedures to solve tasks.

Curriculum Overview

The curriculum for Phase 3 is divided into several key activities, focal experiences, and small group tasks. These are designed to be hands-on and interactive, encouraging students to engage deeply with the material and concepts.

Phase 3 Timeline

Each CRRAFT phase lasts approximately 6 weeks, though the timeline is flexible to accommodate classroom needs.

  • Week 1: Introduce focal robot (Kibo), small group activities, and centers.
  • Week 2-5: Continue building and exploring focal experiences, small groups, and centers.
  • Week 6: Combine learning and projects to culminate the phase.

Focal Experiences

Phase 3 includes several focal experiences designed to introduce and reinforce key concepts:

  • My Name is KIBO: Exploring hardware/software and modularity.
  • How Far Does Robot Go? Debugging and representation using robots.
  • Design/Build a City: Collaborating to design and build an interactive robot city.
  • Hokey Pokey: Using modularity and representation to program KIBO.

Centers

Phase 3 centers provide a variety of hands-on learning opportunities, each focused on a different aspect of the curriculum:

  • Art Center: Creating plans for cities and designing KIBO stages.
  • Construction Zone: Building structures for cities and exploring modularity.
  • Books/Library: Reading about building, machines, and cities.
  • Dramatic Play: Engaging in role-play with robot dress-up materials and community helper outfits.
  • Puzzles and Games: Solving puzzles that incorporate modularity and debugging.
  • iPad/Computer: Interactive games that reinforce concepts of shapes, building, and coding.
  • Writing: Creating maps and signs for the robot city.
