CRRAFT Phase 2: We Can Do STEM

Using strategic approaches to solve challenging problems

Introduction

Phase 2 is all about using strategic approaches to solve challenging problems. In this phase, students will explore the design process, delve into hardware and software interactions, and engage in debugging to solve issues that arise during their activities. These experiences are designed to help children see that STEM is accessible to everyone and can be applied to everyday life.

Phase 2: Powerful Ideas

The following powerful ideas are central to Phase 2:

  • Design Process: A structured approach to problem-solving, helping students tackle complex challenges by breaking them down into manageable steps.
  • Hardware/Software: Understanding the interaction between physical components and the software that controls them is essential for exploring how robots function.
  • Debugging: Identifying and solving problems that arise during the design and implementation process is crucial to success in STEM activities.

Learning Standards

  • PK.AL.CB.14 - Demonstrate a willingness to collaborate with others to solve a problem.
  • PK.AL.CT.8 - Seek additional clarity to further own knowledge by asking relevant questions.
  • PK.SL.PKI.5 - Create representations and extensions of experiences or stories through various mediums, and discuss them with others.
  • PK.ETS2.01a - Recognize that tools have specific characteristics that determine their use.

Phase 2 Goal

Children will understand that using the design process helps us solve complex problems in our daily lives. This means that STEM is everywhere and accessible to everyone!

Small Group Activities

Robot vs. Robot

In this activity, students will explore the nature and functions of different robots. They will compare and contrast various robots, understanding what makes them unique and how they operate.

[Image of Robot vs. Robot Activity]

Key Questions: What makes something a robot? How can we represent the actions of a robot on paper? What differences and similarities do we notice between different robots?

Materials Needed: Robot Mouse, Botley, Directional cards, Chart paper, markers

Robot Prototype

In this session, students will design and build their own robots. They will use the design process to plan and create a prototype, considering the functions and characteristics of their robots.

[Image of Robot Prototype Activity]

Key Questions: What can your robot do? How will it function? What materials will you use to build your robot?

Materials Needed: Loose parts, recycled materials, Design Process poster

Welcome Home, Robot

This activity focuses on designing homes for the robots that the students have created. They will think about what their robots need and how they will interact with their environment.

[Image of Welcome Home, Robot Activity]

Key Questions: How will your robot enter and exit its home? What shapes and materials will you use in your design?

Materials Needed: Robot prototypes, Classroom robots, Design materials

Problems We Can Solve (ABC Tree)

Inspired by the book "Chicka Chicka Boom Boom," students will collaborate to solve a problem by designing and constructing an ABC Tree. They will use the design process to plan, build, and debug their structures.

[Image of ABC Tree Activity]

Key Questions: What is the challenge or problem in the book? How can we represent our plan for building? What steps can we take to fix any issues that arise?

Materials Needed: "Chicka Chicka Boom Boom" book or recording, Building materials (wooden cubes, foam blocks, magnet letters), Paper, writing utensils

Focal Texts and Extensions

The following books are central to Phase 2 and can be used to deepen students' understanding of STEM concepts:

  • The Astronaut with a Song for the Stars: This book tells the story of Dr. Ellen Ochoa, the first female Latina astronaut, and how she made her dreams come true. Use this book to discuss the importance of perseverance and the design process in achieving goals.
  • Robots, Robots, Everywhere: This book introduces students to the many roles robots play in our lives, from helping on the farm to vacuuming the house. It supports understanding of hardware and software.
  • Pete the Cat: Robot Pete: Pete the Cat builds a robot to play with when his friends are busy. This story can be used to explore the design process, debugging, and the importance of communication in robot programming.
  • Computer Decoder: This book highlights the life of Dorothy Vaughan, a groundbreaking African American computer scientist. It can be used to discuss who can do STEM and the design process in problem-solving.

Essential Questions

These essential questions guide the exploration and activities in Phase 2:

  • What is a robot? What does it do? Where do we see robots in our lives?
  • How do we break up a task into iterative steps to solve a problem?
  • Who is a computer scientist? How do they solve problems?

Design Process Overview

The design process is a series of steps that help us solve complex problems in strategic and creative ways. It is essential to remember that this process is iterative; we can repeat steps as needed to refine our solutions. This approach encourages students to embrace mistakes as learning opportunities and to keep trying until they find the best solution.

[Image of Design Process]

Steps in the Design Process:

  1. Identify the Problem: Understand the problem you need to solve.
  2. Brainstorm Solutions: Think of different ways to solve the problem.
  3. Plan: Develop a plan for your solution, considering materials and steps needed.
  4. Create a Prototype: Build a test model of your solution.
  5. Test and Evaluate: Test your prototype to see if it solves the problem. If not, go back and make improvements.
  6. Share: Present your solution to others, gather feedback, and make any necessary adjustments.
